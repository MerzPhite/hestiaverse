# Prompt: Research new online dangers to children (for HESTIA VERSE)

Use this prompt when searching the internet or using an AI/research tool to find **new content** to add to HESTIA VERSE. It keeps research focused, recent, and categorised so we can filter on the site.

---

## Writing style

- **No em dashes.** Do not use em dashes (—) in titles, summaries, or body copy. Use commas, full stops, or short sentences so the writing reads like normal human writing.
- **"(30 seconds)" is for our internal rule only.** When we say "What it is (30 seconds)" we mean: keep that section to a short, scannable paragraph. Do **not** include the phrase "(30 seconds)" on the page itself or in the visible heading. The site shows "What it is" only.

---

## Your task

Search for **current dangers to children online** (apps, platforms, trends, or harms) that caregivers and educators should know about. For each finding, produce a short guideline suitable for turning into a site article/card: clear title, plain-language summary, and categories so parents can filter.

## Recency — last year, still relevant

- Focus on dangers that have **reports, articles, or official mentions in the last 12 months** (from today’s date).

- **Exclude** things that were a problem only years ago and are no longer relevant (e.g. a platform that shut down long ago with no current copycats, or a trend that has clearly ended). If in doubt, prefer “still in the news or still live in the last year.”

## Specificity — no vague articles

- **Name the thing.** Every finding must be about a **named** app, **named** trend, or **named** challenge (e.g. "the Blackout Challenge", "the Nyquil chicken trend", "BeReal", "Gas app"). Do **not** write generic articles like "a wave of TikTok dance challenges" or "increasingly dangerous stunts" without naming the exact trend, app, or challenge.
- **Only real, verifiable things.** Do **not** invent or assume a challenge or trend exists. If you cannot find clear recent coverage (news, safety orgs, law enforcement) for a specific named challenge or trend, choose a different topic. Do not make up names like "the Snapchat ghost emoji challenge" or similar unless you have a real source.
- **Concrete details.** In "What it is" and "Why it's dangerous", include at least one concrete detail: the exact name, a specific incident or report (e.g. "reported by the CDC in 2022"), or a specific harm (e.g. "linked to X hospitalisations").
- **Concrete parent steps.** "What parents should do today" must be 2–4 **concrete, doable** steps. Good: "Search your child's TikTok for [specific challenge name]"; "Turn off DMs from non-friends in Instagram settings"; "Bookmark the NCMEC CyberTipline." Bad: "Monitor your child's activity"; "Talk about risks"; "Encourage them to think critically."

## Required post structure

Every new post must follow this structure so parents get consistent, actionable information.

### Example post format

| Field | Example |
|-------|--------|
| **Title** | ⚠️ New TikTok “Shadow Game” challenge spreading among teens |
| **What it is** (keep it short; we call this "30 seconds" in our rule only; do not show "(30 seconds)" on the page) | Short explanation: what the trend/app/risk is in plain language. |
| **Why it’s dangerous** | Bullying / self-harm / privacy risk / addiction / grooming / sextortion / scams etc. Pick the main harm(s). |
| **Which kids are affected** | Ages 10–13, mainly TikTok users. Or: “Teens 13–17 on Discord and gaming.” Be specific where possible. |
| **What parents should do today** | Concrete steps, e.g.: Check TikTok search history; Disable X setting; Have this conversation. |
| **Severity rating** | **Low** / **Medium** / **High** — so parents can prioritise. See severity guidelines below. |
| **Platform tags** | TikTok / Discord / Roblox / Instagram / Monkey / etc. (used for filtering and labels). |

When you draft a finding, fill in every row above.

### Severity and major platforms

Use **High** only when there is **documented serious harm or major enforcement**: e.g. FTC/regulatory ban, app removed from stores, law enforcement warnings, widespread sextortion or grooming with recent enforcement, or clear link to deaths/serious injury. Do not use High just because a platform is big or "risky in general."

Use **Medium** for: significant risk with recent reports or regulatory attention; scams or harm with good evidence but no ban or major enforcement; or **major established platforms** (Meta/Threads, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, etc.) where the risk is real but not in the "High" category above. For Meta, Google, Apple, and other large platforms, **default to Medium** unless there is specific recent enforcement, lawsuit, or documented serious harm that clearly justifies High.

Use **Low** for: emerging or lower-impact issues; general awareness topics; or risks that are real but less acute. The **What parents should do today** section should be 2–4 concrete, doable steps.

## Output format for each finding

For every item you suggest, provide all of the following so it can be turned into a post that matches the structure above:

1. **Title** — Short, clear; use ⚠️ for urgent/trending if appropriate (e.g. “⚠️ New TikTok ‘Shadow Game’ challenge spreading among teens” or “App Name — one-line description”).
2. **What it is:** One short paragraph: what it is in plain language. (We use "30 seconds" in this prompt only. Do not put "(30 seconds)" on the page.)
3. **Why it’s dangerous** — Main harm(s): e.g. bullying, self-harm, privacy risk, addiction, grooming, sextortion, scams.
4. **Which kids are affected** — Age range and main platforms (e.g. “Ages 10–13, mainly TikTok” or “Teens 13–17 on Discord and Roblox”).
5. **What parents should do today** — 2–4 concrete steps (e.g. “Check TikTok search history”; “Disable X setting”; “Have this conversation”).
6. **Severity rating** — **Low** | **Medium** | **High**.
7. **Suggested section** — One of: `In focus: specific platforms` | `Emerging & new` | `Ongoing areas of risk` | `What you can do`. If it’s a new kind of content, say “New section: [name]”.
8. **Labels / platform tags** — Space-separated labels for filtering (e.g. `app stranger-chat grooming sextortion`). Include platform names as tags where relevant (TikTok, Discord, Roblox, etc.) so they align with our Categories.
9. **Source / recency** — At least one article or report from the last 12 months (title + URL or date), so we can verify it’s current.

Example:

```markdown
**Title:** ⚠️ New TikTok “Shadow Game” challenge spreading among teens  
**What it is:** Short explanation. (Do not display "(30 seconds)" on the site.)  
**Why it’s dangerous:** Bullying / self-harm / privacy risk.

```
